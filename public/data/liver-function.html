Understanding Your Liver Function Test Results

Introduction: Liver Function Tests (LFTs) measure various enzymes and proteins in your blood to assess liver health and function.

Systemic View of Impacts:

Organ/System Potential Impact
Liver Abnormal results may indicate liver disease, inflammation, or damage
Digestive System Liver dysfunction can affect digestion and nutrient absorption
Blood Liver issues can affect blood clotting and detoxification processes

Interpreting Your Results:

Heuristic for Negative Results: Abnormal LFT results may indicate liver disease, hepatitis, or bile duct issues. It's important to follow up with your healthcare provider for further evaluation.

Heuristic for Positive Results: Normal LFT results indicate healthy liver function and effective detoxification.

Lifestyle Actions:

  • Diet: Eat a balanced diet with adequate protein and avoid excessive alcohol to support liver health.
  • Avoid Toxins: Limit exposure to harmful chemicals and medications that can affect liver function.
  • Regular Monitoring: Regular liver function tests, especially if you have a history of liver issues or risk factors.
