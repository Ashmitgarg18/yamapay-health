Understanding Your Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) Test Results

Introduction: The Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) test measures the level of PSA in your blood, which can help detect prostate cancer and other prostate-related conditions.

Systemic View of Impacts:

Organ/System Potential Impact
Prostate Elevated PSA levels may indicate prostate cancer, benign prostatic hyperplasia, or prostatitis
Urinary System Prostate issues can affect urinary function, causing symptoms like frequent urination or difficulty urinating

Interpreting Your Results:

Heuristic for Negative Results: Elevated PSA levels may indicate prostate cancer or other prostate conditions. Follow-up with your healthcare provider for further evaluation.

Heuristic for Positive Results: Normal PSA levels suggest a low risk of prostate cancer, but regular screening is still important for early detection.

Lifestyle Actions:

  • Diet: Eat a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and healthy fats to support prostate health.
  • Exercise: Engage in regular physical activity to maintain overall health and reduce cancer risk.
  • Regular Screening: Regular PSA testing, especially if you are at higher risk for prostate cancer due to age or family history.
