Understanding Your Electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG) Results

Introduction: An Electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG) test measures the electrical activity of your heart, providing insights into heart health and detecting heart-related conditions.

Systemic View of Impacts:

Organ/System Potential Impact
Heart Abnormal results may indicate arrhythmias, heart disease, or heart attack
Blood Vessels Can detect poor blood flow to the heart (ischemia)
Lungs Certain heart issues can affect lung function and oxygenation

Interpreting Your Results:

Heuristic for Negative Results: Abnormal ECG results may indicate arrhythmias, heart disease, or ischemia. Follow-up with your healthcare provider for further evaluation.

Heuristic for Positive Results: Normal ECG results suggest a healthy heart rhythm and function, with no immediate signs of heart disease.

Lifestyle Actions:

  • Diet: Eat a heart-healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  • Exercise: Engage in regular physical activity to improve cardiovascular health.
  • Quit Smoking: Smoking increases the risk of heart disease. Quitting can improve your heart health.
